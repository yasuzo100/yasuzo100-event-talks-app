Global financial markets are experiencing significant volatility due to the escalating US-Israel war with Iran. Oil prices have surged, with Brent crude briefly rising above $84 a barrel, leading to concerns about inflation and global economic growth.

Stock markets worldwide have reacted negatively to the geopolitical tensions. The Dow experienced a significant drop, and Asian stocks, including South Korea's KOSPI, saw sharp declines. Defense stocks, however, have seen gains.

Other notable financial news includes:
* The UAE non-oil private sector expanded at its fastest pace in 12 months during February.
* Japan's services sector expanded for the eleventh consecutive month in February.
* In India, 80% of stocks are in a bear market, and the rupee hit a record low against the USD.
* Unemployment is projected to reach 5.3% this year, with a worrying rise in young jobless individuals.
* Paramount+ and HBO Max are reportedly set to merge into a single streaming service.
